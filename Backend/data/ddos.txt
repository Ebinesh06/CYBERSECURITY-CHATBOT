A Distributed Denial of Service (DDoS) attack is a cyber attack where multiple systems flood a server, website, or network with excessive traffic, making it unavailable to users.

Attackers often use botnets, which are networks of infected computers, to generate large volumes of traffic simultaneously.

DDoS attacks can target websites, online services, or entire networks, causing downtime, financial loss, and disruption of services.

There are different types of DDoS attacks, including volumetric attacks (overwhelming bandwidth), protocol attacks (exploiting server resources), and application-layer attacks (targeting specific applications).

To defend against DDoS attacks, organizations can use firewalls, rate limiting, traffic filtering, load balancing, and cloud-based protection services.
