Phishing is a cyber attack where attackers trick users into revealing sensitive information such as passwords, credit card details, or personal data.

Attackers often send fake emails, messages, or create fake websites that look like legitimate services such as banks or social media platforms.

These messages usually create urgency, such as "Your account will be blocked" or "Click here to verify immediately".

Common signs of phishing include suspicious links, spelling mistakes, unknown senders, and requests for confidential information.

To prevent phishing attacks, users should verify URLs, avoid clicking unknown links, enable two-factor authentication, and never share sensitive information through email or messages.
